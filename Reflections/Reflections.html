General Rules

 • The events marked (i) are individual events and that marked (g) are group events.
 • Timing will be strictly followed as per the schedule.
 • The school authorities are requested to hand over the Registration form and the overall registration fees with the names of the participants
   only to the organizers who will personally come to the school to collect the above with theRotaract identity.
 • Every school must be represented by a teacher as the manager of the school team. In case of any dispute only the manager/Principal has the 
   authority to question the organizers. Any feedback from the parents is to be informed through their particular school representatives or managers.
 • All the participants must register within the time mentioned in the schedule.
 • It is the responsibility of the manager to register the students during the registration process.
 • Each school shall be given a slot number for the fair and smooth conduction of the event.
 • It is the responsibility of the manager to make sure the participant is present at the right time for an event.
 • Prizes will be distributed in the valediction, at the end of the second day by eminent personalities.
 • Kindly note that the students willing to participate in more than one event do not select the events with overlapping time.
 • Students and the manager of the school are requested to note the scheduling of all the events at the time of registration to prevent any kind of confusion.
 • Registration for group events after the submission of the participation list will not be entertained.

Category - A

Events Rules
1. FANCY DRESS (i)
 
• Two participants per school.
• Time: 2 minutes per participant.
• Participants will be judged based on their outfits and presentation. More weightage
  is given to creativity and costume.
2. POEM RECITATION WITH ACTION DANCE (i)
 
• Two participants per school.
• Time limit is 2 minutes.
• Poem can be in English, Kannada or Hindi.
• Only one poem is to be recited.
• Poem must be submitted to the event in-charge prior to the event.
3. MEMORY GAME (i) 
• Two participants per school.
• 30 objects will be displayed.
• 4 minutes will be given to observe the objects.
• 10 minutes to list out the displayed objects.
4. BEST HANDWRITING (English, Kannada)(i)
 
• Two participants per school (ONE PARTICIPANT per language).
• Time: 30 minutes.
• A participant cannot participate in more than one language.
• Participants will be given a printed passage which they will have to copy into a
sheet provided by the organizers.
5. HOW WELL CAN YOU DRAW (i)
 
• Three participants per school.
• Time: 1 hour.
• Outline of picture will be given and the participant has to redraw and colour it.
• Stationeries should be brought by the participants.
• Sheets will be provided by organizers.
6. STORY NARRATION (i) 
• Two participants per school.
• Time: 2 minutes.
• Stories can be in English or Kannada.
• Reading from any written material is not allowed.
• Participants will be judged based on the content and presentation of the story.
7. PAINTING WITHOUT USING A BRUSH(i)
 
• One participant per school.

• Participants can choose the topic.
8. ORIGAMI(i)
 
• Two participants per school.
• Time: 1 hour.
• Craft sheets & stationeries should be brought by the participants.
• Participants are supposed to do the craft in the allotted time (1 hour).
9. MOSAIC ART(i)
 
• Two participants per school.
• Time: 1 hour.
• Sheets & stationeries should be brought by the participants.

Events Rules
1. MOTION PICTURE (i) 
• Two participants per school.
• Time: 90 minutes.
• The participants are supposed to sketch a series of drawings in the pages such
that when continuously flipped it must appear as if the sketch is in motion.

2. SPELL BEE (i) 
• Two participants per school.
• Time: 20 minutes.
• It consists of two rounds: First, a written round and selected participants are
eligible for the final round, which is oral round.
• Prelims question paper consists of sentences with an underlined word which is
miss-spelt and the participants will have to correct those miss-spelt words.
• In oral round, participants have to spell the words asked.
3. QUIZ (g)

• Time:45 minutes

Eg: How many months have 28 days?
Ans: All 12 months.
5. COLLAGE (g)

• Two participants per school (1 male and 1 female).

10. SHIPWRECK(i) 
• One participant per school.
• It is a writing event.
• Time: 30 minutes.
• You are given the role of Superman/ Rocky Bhai/ Narendra Modi etc. The ship is 
sinking, and the Captain has only one Life-Jacket left. You are on the ship with
other famous celebrities/ fictional characters/ hypothetical characters. Sink into
the given character and try to escape from the sinking ship by using your
impressive, convincing skills and prove that you deserve the only Life-jacket.The
participants are judged on content, humour, valid points.
11. BLIND ARTIST(g)
 
• One player is given a picture of an object or word. The person doesn't know what it is 
    and he has to describe the image using words that will help in identifying the object. The 
    other person draws the object on the basis of the given verbal description and their own 
    guesswork.
• Two teams per school (Two participants per team) 
• The time duration will be given at the event itself.
12. TATTOOING(i)

• Time: 1 hour.

second participant.
